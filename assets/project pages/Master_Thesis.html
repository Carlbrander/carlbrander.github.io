Project Overview

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies generalize well, but they are typically trained on robot datasets that lack explicit spatial supervision. This thesis, carried out at the Microsoft Spatial AI Lab and ETH Zurich, introduces a low-cost, post-collection pseudo-labeling pipeline that augments monocular robot datasets with spatial supervision.

By co-training the vision-language backbone on pseudo-labeled spatial modalities, namely 3D bounding boxes, depth, and spatial visual question answering (VQA), before policy learning, the method injects geometry-aware supervision while staying compatible with standard VLA training recipes. Co-training on this spatially annotated data improves downstream task success rates by up to 8.8 points on SIMPLER and 4.1 points on LIBERO over a matched baseline.

The results presented here reflect only the final 2.5 months of the thesis.

Pseudo-Spatial Annotation Pipeline

The core contribution is a modular, non-learned annotation pipeline that turns a single monocular RGB frame into spatial supervision without depth sensors or manual 3D labeling. It chains three foundation models: Qwen3-VL for open-set scene parsing, SAM 3 for instance masks, and MoGe-2 for monocular geometry.

  • Objects are detected, segmented, and lifted into 3D using pseudo-metric mono-depth.
  • Gravity-aligned, minimum-volume oriented 3D boxes are fit with a RANSAC surface-alignment stage.
  • Metric scale is recovered by aligning point-cloud object heights with VLM-provided size priors.
  • Outputs are exported as reusable modalities: 3D boxes, depth maps, masks, normals, and point clouds.

The 3D bounding-box pseudo-labeling pipeline: VLM open-set proposals, grounded segmentation masks, and monocular depth are combined to fit gravity-aligned 3D boxes.

Three Spatial Pseudo-Datasets

From BridgeData V2 and Fractal (Open-X) robot data, the pipeline generates three complementary pseudo-datasets, each spanning 5.7 million images and all transformed into a unified VQA-style format for interleaved VLM co-training:

  • Spatial VQA: ~320 million questions covering distances, relations, size, and localization.
  • 3D Bounding Boxes: ~340 million questions, tokenized into a shared 1024-bin location vocabulary.
  • Depth: VQ-VAE depth tokens compressing each depth map into 256 discrete codes.

The three spatial pseudo-datasets generated from BridgeData V2 and Fractal robot data, all cast into a VQA-style format for co-training.

Architecture & Training

A two-stage recipe first co-trains the VLM backbone (Qwen2.5-VL-7B) on the pseudo-spatial data with a standard teacher-forced cross-entropy loss, then optimizes the downstream VLA policy. The co-trained VLM is turned into a VLA by attaching a modular flow-matching action head, following VLM4VLA, where action tokens cross-attend to the VLM token sequence at each action-head layer.

  • Four backbone variants: 3D-box only, depth only, spatial-VQA only, and all three jointly.
  • A soft Gaussian CE-loss with an annealed sigma gives a denser signal for the coherent 3D location tokens.
  • Joint variant uses a 55% / 35% / 10% mixture of 3D-box, depth, and spatial-VQA data.

Results & Impact

Across robotic manipulation benchmarks, spatial co-training consistently improves downstream success over a matched baseline. Using all three modalities yields +8.8 points on SIMPLER and +4.1 points on LIBERO. Ablations show depth supervision is the most consistent single-modality gain, while combining modalities gives the best cross-benchmark balance.

These findings show that inexpensive pseudo-spatial annotations can effectively scale VLA pretraining and complement recent co-training strategies in robot learning, without changing the underlying policy-learning recipe.
